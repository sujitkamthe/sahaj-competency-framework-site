## Mindset

"My success is measured by how effective my team becomes without my constant presence."

**The question you're trusted to answer:** "How do we make this team effective?"

## Nature of Impact

- Single team (end-to-end)
- Capability multiplication
- Delivery + people + client outcomes

## Explicit Expectation

- Leads one team
- Owns team health, delivery, consulting quality, and technical bar

---

# Technical Delivery

## Expectations

- Defines technical direction for teams or programs
- Establishes shared patterns, libraries, and standards
- Introduces platforms or accelerators reused across teams
- Improves developer productivity at scale
- Prevents systemic technical debt across projects
- Reviews and influences designs beyond own team
- Drives large-scale refactoring or modernization initiatives
- Balances innovation with platform stability
- Anticipates future technical needs and risks
- Aligns technical strategy with business direction
- Mentors others in advanced engineering concepts
- Raises the overall engineering quality bar
- Acts as a technical escalation point

## Self-Assessment

- Has my work eliminated recurring technical problems?
- Are multiple teams benefiting from my decisions?
- Do my technical choices scale beyond a single engagement?

---

# Consulting

## Expectations

- Leads solutioning for complex, high-ambiguity engagements
- Handles unclear or shifting goals with confidence
- Aligns multiple stakeholders toward a shared direction
- Anticipates stakeholder needs before they are stated
- Coaches others in consulting and problem-framing skills
- Navigates politics and power dynamics effectively
- Builds long-term stakeholder trust
- Shapes engagement direction beyond immediate scope
- Represents Sahaj as a trusted advisor

## Self-Assessment

- Do I influence direction beyond my formal role?
- Are others learning consulting skills by working with me?
- Can I operate effectively in politically complex environments?
